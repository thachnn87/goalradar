# QUALIFICATION_CONTEXT — DATA-18WC.MATCH-STORY

**Date:** 2026-06-26
**Status:** IMPLEMENTED ✅

---

## Purpose

Automatically explain how teams arrived at each match — group winner, runner-up, best third, host nation, etc. Never hardcoded.

---

## Current Implementation

Qualification context is surface-level today: the WC_GROUP template references the group stage and qualification push, and the WC_KNOCKOUT template references the teams advancing/being eliminated. Explicit "how they qualified" sentences (e.g. "Won Group C", "Runner-up Group H") are available via the qualification engine in `src/lib/wc-qualification.ts` and are surfaced on team and group pages.

---

## Qualification Status Labels

| Status | Meaning | Displayed as |
|---|---|---|
| `QUALIFIED` | Mathematically through | "Qualified" (green) |
| `ELIMINATED` | Mathematically out | "Eliminated" (red) |
| `THIRD_PLACE_CONTENDER` | In best-8 race | "3rd-Place Race" (yellow) |
| `UNDECIDED` | Still in contention | "In Contention" (gray) |

## How Teams Qualify for WC 2026 Knockout

| Method | Teams | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Group winner | 12 | 1st place in each of 12 groups |
| Group runner-up | 12 | 2nd place in each of 12 groups |
| Best third-place | 8 | 8 of 12 third-placed teams (sorted by pts → GD → GF) |

Total advancing to Round of 32: **32 teams**

## Future Enhancement (not yet in engine)

The Story Engine context could optionally accept a `qualificationJourney` string to surface how each team arrived. Example:

```
${homeS} arrived as winners of Group E.
${awayS} qualified as the best third-placed team from Group H.
```

This would require passing the `QualificationContext` (from `wc-qualification.ts`) into the `StoryContext` at snapshot/page level.
